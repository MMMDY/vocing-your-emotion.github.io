Voicing Your Emotion:
Integrating Emotion and Identity in Cross-Modal 3D Facial Animations

Wenfeng Song, Zhenyu Lv, Xuan Wang, Xia Hou
Computer School, Beijing Information Science and Technology University
Paper PDF Method Results

Speech + Identity Prompt + Emotion ⟶ expressive 3D talking face animation

Abstract

This project page summarizes the paper "Voicing Your Emotion". The method addresses a key gap in speech-driven 3D face animation: models with accurate lip-sync often fail to preserve rich emotion and identity.

The framework takes speech, prompt words, and emotion conditions as input, then generates a controllable 3D talking face. It combines Text2Avatar, emotion-identity feature fusion, and an autoregressive generator with motion priors.

Method Overview

Fig.2 shows the full pipeline: speech is encoded by Wav2Vec2.0, prompt text is converted to a personalized template via Stable Diffusion + Img2FLAME, emotion and identity are fused in temporal features, and the autoregressive generator predicts final 3D facial motion.

Core Modules

  • Text2Avatar (Sec 3.3): Generates a customizable face template from prompt words.
  • Emotion-Identity Fusion (Sec 3.4): Injects emotion and identity controls into sequential motion representation.
  • Avatar Generator (Sec 3.5): Uses quantized priors and autoregression to synthesize realistic motion sequences.
  • Key Points Loss (Sec 3.6): Strengthens dynamic movement in important facial regions for better expressiveness.

Core Experimental Results

Quantitative metrics on reconstructed data: Ours (LME 1.2070 / AVE 1.2528), compared with FaceFormer (1.2071 / 1.2857) and CodeTalker (1.3039 / 1.2637). Ablation also shows clear gains from key-point supervision: LME improves from 1.2931 to 1.2070 and AVE improves from 1.3604 to 1.2528.

User study reports strong preference for our method: +84.6% and +52.4% on facial dynamism against FaceFormer and CodeTalker. Against the model without Lkey, gains are +63.6% (dynamism) and +52.2% (lip-sync quality). Emotion-expression gain is +58.7% versus the model without emotion-identity fusion.
